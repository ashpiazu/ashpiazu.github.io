Published Articles

After Years of Red Ink, Grass May Soon Be Greener For North Florida Municipal Golf Courses

One of my first published articles for WUFT News, the story focuses on municipal golf courses in North Central Florida. I contributed reporting for the information on the Palatka Golf Club and took the article photograph. After operating at a deficit for years, the money-losing golf course finally began to chip away at its debt. I had a lot of fun working on this story. I had the opportunity to ride along with five golfers while they played nine holes. I learned a lot about golf that day, but more importantly I gained valuable hands-on reporting experience.

Florida's Animal Shelter Law Still All Bark, No Bite

This is by far the most extensive article I've ever reported and written. I filed public records requests to 12 counties in North Centra Florida to see if they were complying with a recent state law aimed at making animal shelter records more accessible to the public. Between filing requests, conducting interviews and visiting animal shelters, the article took me close to three weeks to complete. I believe the hard work paid off, I'm very proud of the finished product.

Design Work

Designing a UF Football T-Shirt

Showcasing my gator pride, I designed a UF football T-shirt for a class assignment. We were tasked with keeping cost and adaptability in mind while designing our T-shirts. Frustated that many UF T-shirts only come in orange, blue or white, I chose to design a shirt that would look good printed on a variety of colors. I also photoshopped the design onto stock photographs of models wearing different color shirts to help show the possible color schemes.

Designing a Magazine Spread

I created the infographic, wrote the article and designed the magazine spread for a visual journalism project. The assignment was to create an infographic to help better explain a national story. I chose to write about the controversy over Makers Mark announcing they were going to add more water to their traditional bourbon recipe. The infographic shows the minimal difference between the alcohol content in the original Makers Mark recipe versus the watered-down recipe. The original recipe was only slightly more potent by two standard drinks. Interested in reading the story? Download the full magazine spread.
